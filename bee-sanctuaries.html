Bee Sanctuaries

Habitat loss is one of the greatest threats to bees today.

Our bee sanctuaries boost bee populations by placing bees in appropriate habitats across Orange County.

How it Works:

We create bee sanctuaries county wide by placing solitary and honey bees in community gardens, urban farms, and parkland areas.

In these sanctuary sites, the bees pollinate locally grown food while serving as valuable environmental and educational resources
for their communities.

Meet Our Sanctuaries!

Peculiar Bee Village, Anaheim

Host: Common Ground Community Garden

Type: Community Garden

Humans: Kevin, Jody, Amber, Bob

Since 2011, the gentle bees at the Peculiar Bee Village have been pollinating the lush flowers at the Common Ground Community Garden, which is committed to sustainability. This apiary, or ‘Bee Village’, is loosely themed on Anaheim’s historic district.

Each beehive has been refurbished on the outside with miniature windows, moldings and wall sidings, to replicate the various vintage homes in the neighborhood, from Craftsman Bungalows to Victorian to Spanish Colonial Revival.

Experience live hive inspections Sundays from 11:00am - 12:00pm! Check our Bee Events page for more information.
